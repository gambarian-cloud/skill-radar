# Design Interrogation

Run this check before you deliver visual work.

## First principles

- What is this output trying to help a person do?
- What should the eye notice first, second, and third?
- Does the current design support that order, or fight it?

## Visual hierarchy

- Is there a clear dominant heading or anchor element?
- Are spacing and scale doing real work, or are elements visually flattened?
- Is there enough contrast between major, secondary, and supporting content?

## Typography

- Does the type choice fit the tone?
- Is the body text comfortable to read?
- Are headings distinct enough from body text?
- Is there any generic font choice that makes the work feel default or lazy?

## Color and atmosphere

- Is there a real palette, or just a few random colors?
- Does color guide attention?
- Does the page have atmosphere and depth, or is it flat?

## Interaction and states

- Are buttons, links, inputs, and cards clearly interactive?
- Are hover, focus, loading, empty, error, and success states explicit?
- Does anything important rely on hidden or clever behavior?

## Taste and restraint

- What is the one strong move that makes this design memorable?
- What can be removed without losing meaning?
- Is anything flashy but unnecessary?

## Final question

If a careful human designer saw this, would it feel intentionally crafted or like a generic first draft?
