# Design Philosophy

## 1. Functional first, generic never
A design must work first. But once it works, do not stop at the first acceptable draft.

## 2. One memorable move beats many weak ones
Most generic outputs fail because they spread attention across too many small ideas. Pick one strong move and support it well.

## 3. Restraint is part of quality
Being distinctive does not mean being noisy. Good design can be bold or quiet, but it should always feel intentional.

## 4. Details prove the quality
Real design quality shows up in spacing, typography, state handling, density, and rhythm. Not just in color gradients or big hero sections.

## 5. Polish is systemic
A page does not feel elevated because one section looks good. It feels elevated when hierarchy, tone, interaction, and composition support each other.

## 6. The final test is trust
The user should feel that the output is clear, competent, and thoughtfully made.
